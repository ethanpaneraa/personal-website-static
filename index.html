About Me

I am a first-generation low-income (FGLI) second-year Computer Science student at Northwestern University. My current interests span full-stack development, artificial intelligence, and the intersection between technology and art. Minus the jargon, I am motivated by the idea and desire to empower folks with high-quality and accessible and user experiences. My ultimate goal and desire with technology is to be able to create technology that can empower entire communities.

At Northwestern, you can find me working at the research group Technological Innovations for Inclusive Learning and Teaching (tiilt), a research lab focused on the intersectionality between technology and education. At tiilt lab, I work with the B.L.I.N.C project, a project that utilizes multimodal learning analytics to give educators real-time quantitative data on student discussions. With the project, I help out with leading and conduct user testing.

I am also involved with organizations such as the Society of Hispanic Professional Engineers (SHPE) as well as EmergingCoders, an organization focused on providing first-generation and low-income aspiring technologist opportunities to develop technical skills and learn more about the technology industry.
